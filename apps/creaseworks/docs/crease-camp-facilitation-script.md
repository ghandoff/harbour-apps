# piloting creaseworks mini with your kids — a facilitation script

A warm, end-to-end run for a grown-up to play **creaseworks mini** with their
children, contribute **attributed** data, and then see how every dashboard +
the behavioural traces light up. Written for Garrett's 6yo + 11yo, but it's the
same for any collective member this week.

The throughline, say it out loud to your kids and hold it yourself:
**nothing here is a test. there are no wrong answers. everything is learning —
by you (the child) and about you (by your grown-up).** A stall, a "wrong" move,
a kid who only wants to *look* — all of it is real, valid data, not failure.

---

## 0 · before the first game (≈5 min, grown-up only) — DO NOT SKIP

If you play before this, the data lands anonymous and we lose it.

1. Open **windedvertigo.com/harbour/creaseworks-mini**.
2. You'll see a green banner: **"👋 grown-ups, start here."** Tap **set up your code →** (or the **☝ for grown-ups** tab on the left edge).
3. **Enter your family code** (the one the collective gave you — e.g. `sunny-fox`). It confirms: *"✓ code saved — sharing + who's-playing are on."* This one code now ties together everything your family does.
4. **Set up who's playing** (anonymous avatars — colour + animal, never names):
   - **🧒 the kids** — add one buddy for your **6yo** and one for your **11yo** (e.g. `teal otter`, `plum owl`). Quietly remember which is which — that mapping stays in your head.
   - **👤 the grown-ups** — add one for **yourself** (e.g. `coral fox`). This is how we tell which grown-up facilitated, across sessions.
5. Done. Two children + one grown-up on the roster, all under your one code.

> Why it matters: the avatar is the *only* within-child key. It's what lets us
> see how **your 6yo** plays vs **your 11yo**, and how each one grows across
> visits — without ever storing a name.

---

## 1 · play a session with your 6yo (≈10–15 min)

Do the two girls **separately** so each one's play is attributed to *her* buddy.

1. From the mini home, tap **let's look!**
2. A **"who's playing? 👋"** card appears — your 6yo taps **her** buddy (teal otter). (If it asks who's the grown-up, that's you — coral fox.)
3. **look** — she hunts for a few materials around the house and taps what she found. *Give minimal instruction — "what can you find?" is enough. Let her lead.*
4. **make** — the app matches a playdate to her stuff (or she picks one). Read the **"read this aloud"** steps to her, then **step back**. When she uses something "wrong," that's the point — don't fix or finish her build.
5. **show** — snap a photo of what she made, and type **her exact words** ("it has three eyes and its name is wobbles" beats a tidy summary). There's a **"talk about it"** reflection prompt — ask it, capture what *changed* for her.
6. Her **"your turn! how was it?"** card — she taps a face (good / great / the best) and "play again?". That's the **kid** voice.
7. Open the **☝ for grown-ups** corner → **"tell us what you saw"** — tick what you actually saw her do (absorbed, kept going after a mistake, made it her own…). That's the **grown-up** voice, attributed to you (coral fox).

## 2 · play a session with your 11yo (≈10–15 min)

Same flow — but at **"who's playing?"** she taps **her** buddy (plum owl). Everything she does now attributes to the 11yo, so later we can compare the two sisters' play side by side. Let her go deeper / weirder — the 11yo will likely take a different path, and that contrast is the gold.

> Tip: try the **same playdate** with both, or let each pick — either way the
> within-child view will show their different repertoires.

---

## 3 · wear the collective hat (≈10 min, optional but valuable)

1. Open **windedvertigo.com/harbour/creaseworks-eval**.
2. Tap **"i'm reviewing it"** (collective) → tap your name (**garrett**).
3. Pick the playdate your girls just did → climb the **five lenses** (mark only what feels salient — nothing is required). This is the **collective** voice that the dashboard pools separately from the kids/grown-ups.

---

## 4 · now watch it all light up

1. **Coherence dashboard** — **windedvertigo.com/harbour/creaseworks-eval/dashboard**
   - **the three streams, kept separate**: 🧒 what the kids felt · 👀 what you saw · 🧭 the collective's lenses.
   - **behavioural signals** card (the **4th stream**): play sessions, **% reached show**, **% look-only**, **children seen** — straight from the passive traces.
   - the **heatmap**, **sprint readiness** (rank input), **where the room splits**, and the qualitative "in their words".
2. **Engagement insights** (designer-only) — **…/creaseworks-eval/insights** (linked at the dashboard bottom)
   - **within-child**: a row per buddy — your 6yo (teal otter) and 11yo (plum owl) — repertoire of play-modes, return cadence, variability. *This is where "how my child learns" lives.*
   - **path-shape**: % reached show, **% look-only (a valid profile, not a deficit)**, breadth-vs-fixation.
   - **cohorts** (family vs class) and the **justice + negative** indicators.

---

## 5 · what to notice + how we iterate

As you look, hold these questions — your answers are the real point of the pilot:

- **Is it meaningful?** Does the within-child view tell you something true about how your 6yo vs 11yo learns? Would *another* parent feel seen by it?
- **Does it reassure?** Does the framing land that **a stall or a look-only session is learning, not failure**? If the dashboards read as "scoring the child," we've drifted — we profile the *play*, never the player.
- **What's missing?** A signal you wanted and couldn't find? A number that misleads? Something that should be celebrated but isn't?
- **For the collective**: is this useful *feedback* (what to build next) — and for families, would it help an adult **see how their child learned**?

Jot these down (or drop them in #crease-camp). They drive the next iteration of
what we **gather, analyse, and visualise** — so the dashboards become genuinely
meaningful for the collective *and* a gift back to the families.
